## **Luck & Risk**

This chapter explains that luck and risk are two sides of the same coin. Every outcome in life is influenced not only by effort and skill, but also by forces outside our control.

The story of Bill Gates shows how luck can shape success. Gates attended one of the very few schools in the world that had a computer in the late 1960s, giving him a massive head start. Gates himself admits that without this opportunity, Microsoft might never have existed. His intelligence and hard work mattered‚Äîbut luck played a crucial role.

At the same time, the story of Gates‚Äô close friend Kent Evans shows the other side: risk. Evans was equally talented and ambitious but died in a mountaineering accident before he could realize his potential. The same odds that gave Gates extraordinary luck gave Evans devastating misfortune.

The chapter argues that we often exaggerate skill when judging success and exaggerate mistakes when judging failure. Successful people are praised for bold decisions, while unlucky people are blamed for poor judgment‚Äîeven when both took similar risks.

History is full of examples where bold actions look like genius only because they worked. If the outcome had been different, the same actions would be called reckless or criminal. This makes learning simple lessons from success stories very dangerous.

Because luck and risk are hard to measure and uncomfortable to acknowledge, people tend to ignore them. But doing so leads to false confidence in success and unnecessary self-blame in failure.

**Key lesson:**

Be humble when things go well, forgiving when things go wrong, and cautious about idolizing or judging others. Focus on broad patterns, not extreme success stories, because extreme outcomes are often driven heavily by luck or risk.
