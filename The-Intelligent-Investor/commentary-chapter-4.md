## **Summary in English**

In Grahamâ€™s time, bond investors faced two main choices: **taxable vs tax-free bonds** and **short-term vs long-term bonds**. Today, there is a third decision: **owning individual bonds or bond funds**.

## **Summary in English**

Savings bonds are **non-marketable**, meaning they cannot be sold to other investors. Redeeming them within five years results in the loss of **three monthsâ€™ interest**.

Inflation-protected **I-bonds** have recently offered yields of around **4%**, making them attractive for investors who can leave their cash untouched for several years.

**Key takeaway:** Defensive investors should not let cash sit idle. By choosing **safe, liquid, and low-risk instruments**, they can modestly increase income while preserving capital and flexibility.

## **English (Easy & Short)**

**Mortgage Securities:**
These pool thousands of home loans and are issued by Fannie Mae or Ginnie Mae. Not Treasury-backed, so higher yield and higher risk. Performance fluctuates with interest rates. Defensive investors should use **low-cost mortgage bond funds** and avoid **individual bonds or CMOs**.

Never buy just for dividends. Company must be strong and price reasonable. Some high-quality stocks may **yield more than Treasuries** after bear markets. Selectively adding them improves income and long-term returns.

**Key takeaway:** Safety, simplicity, and low cost matter more than chasing yield.
