## **TIMING IS NOTHING**

### **Short Summary (Roman Urdu)**

Is hissa ka central message yeh hai ke **market timing aik ghalat khayal hai**. Theory mein lagta hai ke agar investor sahi waqt par stocks khareeday aur mehngay honay par bech de to bohat zyada paisa bana sakta hai. Lekin asal masla yeh hai ke **kisi ko pehle se yeh nahi pata hota ke sab se bure din kaun se honge**. Yeh baat sirf hindsight mein clear hoti hai.

Is ki misaal **Alan Greenspan** ki di gayi hai, jo apne waqt ka bohat bara financial expert tha, lekin phir bhi market ko ghalat samjha. Jaise **S√∏ren Kierkegaard** ne kaha: zindagi ko peeche dekh kar samjha ja sakta hai, lekin jeena hamesha aagay hota hai. ---

### **Short Summary (English)**

This section explains why **market timing does not work in real life**. While it may seem logical to buy stocks when they are cheap and sell when they are expensive, the problem is that **no one can know in advance which days will be the worst or the best**. Such clarity only exists in hindsight.

The text highlights that even top experts like **Alan Greenspan** made confident predictions that turned out to be completely wrong. Studies also show that professional market timers and newsletters generally **underperform simple stock index funds** over time.

As **S√∏ren Kierkegaard** observed, life can only be understood backward but must be lived forward. In investing, hindsight is always clear, but foresight is unreliable. Therefore, for most investors, **market timing is both practically impossible and emotionally exhausting**, making long-term disciplined investing the smarter choice.

### üìå **English Summary**
